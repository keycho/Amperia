# THE LONG DARK — chapters 4–8 (SCRIPT FOR VOICE REVIEW — not implemented)

Written against the LOCKED canon note (Game Bible B7a). The braid: Sable's
wicks (ch1), the Conductor's loop (ch2), the warm Dynamo and the torn page
(ch3) — chapters 4–8 pull the three threads until they turn out to be one
rope. Open canon handled per B7a §6: **ch7 addresses who banked the Dynamo
warm** (the trail, never the name); **ch8 addresses page one** (what it was,
not what the unreadable half says); **who built the Dynamo stays open** —
reserved past this questline, on purpose.

Production notes, called out for review:

  world nouns). Private-dialogue rule applies as ever.

  (the freight lift, the Old Works hall, story markers). They introduce
  one new NPC, **the Liftkeeper**, at the shaft gate. New task type

## CHAPTER 4 — THE DARK ROUNDS
*NPC: Vessa, the Amped Bar. Unlock: ch3 done + Skimming 4.*
*Task: bring Vessa 4 Glowkoi (`gather` glowkoi ×4).*

**Intro**

- **Vessa:** New face, old thirst. Sit anywhere that isn't the end stool. That one's taken, and no, you don't see anybody on it. Sharp eyes. Wrong conclusion.
- **Vessa:** Since you're here — my stew pot's short. Four glowkoi, skimmed fresh, and don't let the canal hear you bragging. Bring them and I'll tell you about the stool.

**Choice**

- *"Who's the stool for?"* → **Vessa:** Somebody who never once sat in it. That's the point of it, which makes no sense to you yet. Koi first. Stories pour better over a burner.
- *"Four koi. Easy."* → *(no reply — straight to send)*

**Send**

- **Vessa:** Mind the wisps out by the slow water. They don't want your koi. They want your attention. Don't give it.

**Task tracker:** `Skim 4 Glowkoi for Vessa's stew`

**Outro**

- **Vessa:** Fat ones. Good. The pot forgives a lot but it doesn't forgive stingy. Right — the stool.
- **Vessa:** This bar was my aunt's pitch during the Dark. No power for the taps, so she poured what poured cold, and on the worst nights she poured what she called the dark rounds — free, no questions, chalk mark on the wall behind. Fourteen months of chalk. Nobody ever came back to square a mark, because she never once looked at the wall while she made it.
- **Vessa:** The end stool was for the Conductor. Every night that tram ran past our lamps, out into all that black, and every night my aunt set a cup at the end stool for when the loop came home. Never once got sat on. The loop always ran late and the Conductor always waved through the window and rang on. Fourteen months, one full cup a night.
- **Vessa:** When it ended, my aunt kept setting the cup. Habit, she said. Same as Sable's wicks. This city runs on habits it refuses to explain. Here — you've earned a real answer and I've given you a stool. Take the chalk too. It's down to a stub, same as everything that mattered back then.

**Keepsake:** `barChalk` — *A STUB OF BAR CHALK — fourteen months of marks nobody collected.*

**Journal:** Vessa's aunt poured dark rounds free through the Long Dark and chalked marks she never counted. The end stool still gets a full cup, for a Conductor who never once had time to sit.

## CHAPTER 5 — THE SOUTH PLATFORM
*NPC: Sable. Unlock: ch4 done + Scavving 10.*
*Task: gather 8 Brass (`gather` brass ×8).*

**Intro**

- **Sable:** Back again, love. Good. I've been sorting the deep crates and my wrists have opinions. Bring me eight good brass fittings — the heaps south of the plaza run rich with them, and there's a reason for that you should hear standing exactly where you're standing.
- **Sable:** Look down. Go on. This plaza — the stones under your boots — this was the dump. The whole south platform was. Fourteen months of a dark city throws a lot away.

**Choice**

- *"They built the plaza on a dump?"* → **Sable:** They built the plaza *out* of one, which is a different and better thing. Cleared it barrow by barrow with their own hands. Everything worth saving got saved. Some of it's still turning up. That's your errand, if you're quick enough to have already guessed.
- *"Eight fittings. Going."* → *(no reply)*

**Send**

- **Sable:** South heaps, love. And if you turn up anything with a wick in it, I'll want to see it before the scales do.

**Task tracker:** `Gather 8 Brass from the south heaps`

**Outro**

- **Sable:** Good fittings. Boiler brass, most of this — off the Old Works, from when the crews came stripping plate to rebuild it. You know that part. Here's the part the records keep flat.
- **Sable:** Clearing a dump is reading a diary backwards. Every layer, the crews turned up how people had lived: lamp after lamp after lamp, burned to the collar. Wick-crates with my mother's knots on them, empty. And near the bottom — my mother swore this on her scales — tools. Good tools, laid out neat under a tarp, *with fresh oil on them.* Fourteen months of dark, and somebody had been keeping their tools oiled. Under the dump. Right about where that machine was idling.
- **Sable:** Nobody claimed them. Nobody ever claimed them. My mother kept one lamp off the top layer instead — unclaimed, like its owner. We light it once a year and we don't say for whom, because we don't know. That's not a sad thing, love. It's a *kept* thing. There's a difference, and by now you know the difference has a story to it.
- **Sable:** Take the lamp this year. My wrists, remember.

**Keepsake:** `unclaimedLamp` — *AN UNCLAIMED LAMP — lit once a year, for nobody they could name.*

**Journal:** The Filament plaza is the old dump, cleared by hand. Near the bottom of it the crews found good tools under a tarp — oiled, tended, fourteen months into the Dark. Nobody ever claimed them.

## CHAPTER 6 — PAST RATING
*NPC: the Liftkeeper (NEW — the shaft gate at the Filament's north edge).
Unlock: ch5 done + Delving 8 + first Underworks descent available (U-block).*
*Task: descend to the Underworks and reach the Old Works boiler hall
(`visit` marker: the hall floor), then return to the Liftkeeper.*

**Intro**

- **the Liftkeeper:** Lamp. Show me your lamp. Rule of the shaft: nobody rides down dark. The dark down there isn't the night kind. It's the *original* kind. The night kind is what leaked out.
- **the Liftkeeper:** You want the Old Works, same as the rest of the story-chasers. Fine. The lift knows the way and I keep the lift honest. Walk the hall, look at what's written there, and come back up and tell me if it says what they all say it says.

**Choice**

- *"What do they all say it says?"* → **the Liftkeeper:** No. It's a chalkboard, not a rumor. You read it with your own lamp or not at all. That's the other rule of the shaft, and I made it up just now, for you.
- *"Send me down."* → *(no reply)*

**Send**

- **the Liftkeeper:** Feed your lamp, count your fuel, and if the wisps drift close, stand still and be boring. Down you go.

**Task tracker:** `Reach the Old Works boiler hall, then ride back up`

**Outro**

- **the Liftkeeper:** So. You read it.
- **the Liftkeeper:** *"RUN HER PAST RATING — SIGNED, ALL OF US."* Chalk, steady hand, and under it every shift-mark in the hall. The seams were thinning. The city was cold. So the whole crew — not one reckless fool, not a villain in the piece, *all of them, signed* — ran the boilers past what the boilers could carry. For months. Every one of those signatures knew.
- **the Liftkeeper:** And one night the hall paid it all back at once. That's the Works Failure. No sabotage, no monster. Wear, shortage, and people doing the brave arithmetic wrong. The kindest wrong sum ever added, maybe. It kept the city warm right up until it put the city in the dark.
- **the Liftkeeper:** I keep the lift honest because of that board. Rules aren't for the machine's sake, Spark. The machine never asks to be run past rating. Take this — the hall floor is cobbled with them. Gauge faces, melted smooth. The needles all read nothing now, which is the most honest a gauge ever gets.

**Keepsake:** `slagGauge` — *A MELTED GAUGE FACE — it finally reads nothing, honestly.*

**Journal:** The Works Failure had no villain. The whole crew signed the chalkboard and ran the boilers past rating to keep a cold city warm — and one night the hall paid it back all at once.

## CHAPTER 7 — THE KEEPER
*NPC: the Charge Warden. Unlock: ch6 done.*
*Task: follow the cold trail in the Underworks — find three keeper's camps
(`visit` markers ×3: bedroll alcove · wick-tin shelf · the tally wall).*

**Intro**

- **the Charge Warden:** You went down. You read the board. Then you already know the question the board doesn't answer: the Works died in one night — so who kept the Dynamo *warm* for fourteen months? A banked stove doesn't bank itself, Spark.
- **the Charge Warden:** Delvers keep finding places down there that aren't ruin. Tended places. I want a witness I trust who isn't me. Walk the cold trail. Three sites. Look with your lamp and your patience, then come tell my ledger what you saw.

**Choice**

- *"You think somebody lived down there?"* → **the Charge Warden:** I think fourteen months is four hundred and change of days, and the machine was warm on every one of them. Living is one word for what that takes.
- *"Three sites. I'll walk it."* → *(no reply)*

**Send**

- **the Charge Warden:** Take fuel to spare. The trail sits beyond where the veins run rich, which tells you something about whoever walked it daily.

**Task tracker:** `Find the three keeper's camps in the Underworks`

**Outro**

- **the Charge Warden:** Tell me. No — let me tell you, and you stop me where I'm wrong. A bedroll, flat as paper, folded square. A shelf tin of wick-stubs — *dipped* wicks, Nightstalls knots. And punched tram tickets in a jar, months of them, all the same leg.
- **the Charge Warden:** You see it now. Say it anyway — no. I'll say it. My ledger, my ink. *The three stories were one story.* Sable's mother dipped the wicks. The Conductor's loop carried them — and carried down whatever else a person needs to live beside a sleeping machine. And somebody at the bottom of that lift burned them, one stub a night, feeding amperite to a warm machine in the original dark, alone, for fourteen months. The stalls, the tram, the stove. One rope, three strands, and the city braided it without one strand knowing the others' names.
- **the Charge Warden:** And the tally wall. Fourteen months of marks — you counted, I know you counted — and beside the last mark, one word. In the maker's script. The same hand as the casing mark, Spark, or near enough that my ledger won't split the difference.
- **the Charge Warden:** I don't know who the keeper was. Nobody's ledger knows. But somebody kept, and the keeping is the fact that matters. Take a stub. Sable will know the knot — and that, I think, should be a conversation you two have without me.

**Keepsake:** `wickStub` — *A BURNED WICK-STUB — one a night, fourteen months, alone.*

**Journal:** Someone lived beside the Dynamo through the whole Long Dark and kept it banked — burning Nightstalls wicks the Conductor's loop carried down. Three stories, one rope. Beside the last tally mark: one word, written in the maker's script.

## CHAPTER 8 — PAGE ONE
*NPC: the Charge Warden, at the Dynamo. Unlock: ch7 done.*
*Task: donate 20 Amperite to the Citywide Charge (`donate` amperite ×20) —
the city thanks its keeper the only way it knows how.*

**Intro**

- **the Charge Warden:** Last entry, Spark. My ledger and I owe you an ending, and a warden pays ledger-debts. But endings cost. Twenty amperite to the meter — not a toll, an *observance*. The keeper fed this machine alone for fourteen months. Today it gets fed in company, and it gets fed loud.
- **the Charge Warden:** Fill the meter. Then stand here, by the north face, where the shadow sits — and I'll open the case I carry and close the story I can close.

**Choice**

- *"And the story you can't close?"* → **the Charge Warden:** Stays open. A city should keep one door it hasn't found the key to. Keeps it humble. Keeps it *lit*, too, in my experience — people tend a mystery better than they tend a certainty.
- *"Twenty. For the keeper."* → *(no reply)*

**Send**

- **the Charge Warden:** The Underworks veins run richest of anywhere, if you're going down for it. Take your lamp. You know why, now — all the way down to the bone, you know why.

**Task tracker:** `Donate 20 Amperite — an observance for the keeper`

**Outro**

- **the Charge Warden:** Feel that. The whole plating, singing. That's the loudest the meter's spoken this season, and it said your name doing it.
- **the Charge Warden:** Now. Page one. Torn, not lost — you've had that much. Here is the rest: it isn't lost because *I carry it.* Every warden does. Folded, sealed, handed down teacher to student with the ledger and one instruction: don't.
- **the Charge Warden:** The first warden tore it out the day the count began. Because page one wasn't a record. It was already there when the first warden opened the first ledger's cover — a leaf of the maker's script, laid inside like a note left on a doorstep. The first warden couldn't read it. Nobody living can. So they tore it from the book — records begin at the re-founding, *nothing* in my ledger gets to be older than the city that keeps it — and on the back, in plain hand, they wrote the only translation the city has ever needed. That side, Spark, I am permitted to show.
- **the Charge Warden:** *"It was warm when we found it. Keep it warm. That's the whole of the job."*
- **the Charge Warden:** Who built the Dynamo, who the keeper was, what the maker's script says on the front of this page — my ledger keeps those the way the bar keeps a stool and the stalls keep dipping wicks: open, tended, and not for solving tonight. Take the rubbing of the back page. You've walked every strand of the rope, Spark. Time you held the knot.

**Keepsake:** `pageOneRubbing` — *A RUBBING OF PAGE ONE (the back) — "Keep it warm. That's the whole of the job."*

**Journal:** Page one was never lost — every warden carries it, torn and sealed, a leaf of the maker's script no one can read. On its back, the first warden's plain hand: it was warm when we found it; keep it warm; that's the whole of the job. Who built it, and who kept it, the city holds open — tended, like everything it loves.
