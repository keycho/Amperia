Field Guide

How the city works

Amperia is an isometric salvage-punk city you scavenge, build, and trade in — a living world strung across the void and lit by the Great Dynamo. This guide covers how to play, how the city pays you, and how $AMP works.

What is Amperia

You are a Spark — one of the people who keep a dark city burning. You gather salvage from the heaps, delve brass and amperite, skim glowkoi from the canals, tune signal from the antenna masts, craft and upgrade your gear, run a shop in the market, and help feed the Great Dynamo that lights the whole place.

It's a cozy, social, open-world game first. The token, $AMP, is the premium layer on top — it buys identity and access, never power. Everything that makes you stronger comes from playing.

The one-line version: gather → sell → spend, and keep the city lit. Everything else is detail.

Getting started

Getting in

Amperia runs on your wallet. Connect any Ethereum wallet (like MetaMask) on Robinhood Chain, sign in, and you're a Spark — no email, no password, nothing spent and nothing leaves your wallet. Your wallet is your account and your entry.

Holding 1,000 $AMP becomes your key to the full city soon. It's not a fee and it's never spent — it simply stays in your wallet as your key. At launch you connect and play; the key activates as the city grows, so you can hold it early and be ready.

Just want to look? Spectate without a wallet — walk the streets and watch the city live, read-only. Connect whenever you're ready to actually play.

Shape your Spark

Every player designs their own Spark — skin, hair, jacket, and flair. This is who the city knows you as, so make one worth remembering. You can change your look later with cosmetics from the Foundry.

Your first hour

The whole game grows out of one simple loop. The city walks you through it the moment you arrive:

GatherFind a glinting junk heap and work it with your Magclaw. It gives Salvage.
SellCarry your Salvage to a Merchant in the Nightstalls and sell it for Bolts.
SpendBuy a Warmcup, better tools, or a tram ride. Now you're moving.

From there it opens up: more resources, more trades, quests from the Dispatcher, your own market stall, and the wider city. The first stretch is generous on purpose — the city pays new Sparks well while you find your feet, then settles into a steady rhythm.

Look for the floating icons. Anything you can interact with shows a symbol above it — a coin for merchants, a claw for gather spots, a wheel for the Fortune Coil. Walk up and press E.

The two currencies

Amperia keeps its game money and its token strictly separate. This is the most important thing to understand, and it's deliberate.

soft currency

Bolts

The city's own currency. You get Bolts only by playing — selling resources, completing quests, running a stall. Bolts buy tools, repairs, consumables, tram rides, and gear upgrades.

Bolts never convert to the token. The game economy stays a game.

hard currency

$AMP

The premium layer, on Robinhood Chain (an Ethereum L2). Fixed supply, liquidity locked — no more can ever be made. $AMP buys cosmetics, memberships, market stall deeds, and other identity and access items.

$AMP never buys power. No stats, no faster gathering, no advantage.

Because the two never mix, playing the game can't mint or move the token, and spending the token can't buy your way to the top of the ladder. They live side by side.

The trades

There are six trades, each with its own tool and its own Mastery track from 1 to 50. You level them by doing them, and higher Mastery opens better nodes, recipes, and cosmetic rewards — never raw power over other players.

The world

Amperia is one connected city of floating districts, linked by the Tramgates. You leave the market to work the edges, and come back to trade.

  • The Filament — the beating heart. The Great Dynamo, the Nightstalls market street, the Ledgerhouse, the Fortune Coil. Where you start.
  • The Stacks — a vertical canyon of towers, rooftop markets and stair-runs. Ride the tram here free from the Filament.
  • More districts — the Tangle, the Terrarium, the Boneyards, the Canals and the Circuit open the city outward over time.

Tram rides between distant districts cost a few Bolts — a small toll that keeps the currency circulating. Your first ride is on the city, and the nearest district is always free, so no new Spark is ever stuck.

The Nightstalls

The Nightstalls are the market street — a row of stalls under strung lights that never quite closes. Two things happen here.

Buy and sell with the Merchants. NPC merchants like Sable buy your resources and sell you goods. Their prices move with real supply and demand across the whole city, so the resource everyone's dumping pays less, and the neglected one pays more. Watching prices is itself part of the game.

Run your own stall. Rent a stall, stock your shop, and it sells while you're offline. Set your prices, come back to a fuller coffer. Every trade, toll and repair is a real, measured economy — published openly in the City Ledger.

Cosmetics & the Foundry

The Cosmetic Foundry is the premium shop, priced in $AMP. Everything in it is style — capes, crowns, coats, wings — and nothing in it affects gameplay. Pure look, no stats, ever.

Cosmetics come in three states, shown plainly:

  • Available — in the Foundry now.
  • Seasonal — leaves when the season ends. When a set is close to leaving, the Foundry shows the date well in advance.
  • Vaulted — a past season's set, retired for good. Vaulted items are shown in the catalog as history and never sold again.

Scarcity here is honest: sets are scarce because of time, announced ahead, never through hidden odds or pressure timers. Amperia has no paid randomness anywhere — you always know exactly what you're getting.

Keeping the city lit

The Great Dynamo lights the city, and it runs on the whole population. Feed it amperite and the Citywide Charge meter climbs — hit the weekly threshold and the whole city literally brightens, the weekend turns festival, and the top contributors wear an untradeable glow.

The city keeps score of who kept it burning. It's a shared goal, not a grind — and it's the closest thing Amperia has to a heartbeat.

The $AMP economy

One wheel, one direction. Nothing is ever printed — the mint is renounced — and every part of the loop points the same way: supply down.

Hold 1,000 $AMPYour key. Held in your wallet, never spent.
Play for BoltsThe city runs on Bolts. Bolts never touch the token.
Spend if you wantCosmetics, memberships, stalls. $AMP only. Identity, never power.
Every spend burns 30%Burned onchain at the till. The other 70% goes to the treasury.
Volume buys backTrading-volume creator rewards fund monthly buybacks: half burned, half to the champions' purse.
The city growsPurses fund refereed finals. New Sparks bring keys. Supply only falls.

Where the money goes

When you spend $AMP in the shop, 30% is burned onchain immediately and 70% goes to the city treasury — a public wallet with two rules: it never sells, and its only outflows are further burns and the champions' purse (the prize reserve that pays refereed Circuit finals and world-firsts, under fixed caps). From the market's side, the treasury's share is as good as gone: it can only ever come back as burns or as prizes in players' hands.

What funds the buybacks

Amperia's creator rewards — the trading-volume fees the token earns on Robinhood Chain — fund an automated monthly buyback. Half of what it buys is burned; half tops up the champions' purse. So the market's own activity feeds the wheel, while in-game spending burns directly. Two independent paths, both pointing supply downward. And because $AMP fair-launched with no mint and no team allocation, none of it can ever be printed back.

Amperia is a game; $AMP is a token you use inside it, not an investment. This page describes how the mechanism works — it makes no promise about price or return, and it never will.

The City Ledger

Every Bolt, trade, toll, burn and buyback is recorded. The City Ledger is the public dashboard where those numbers live — players, Bolts in circulation, Bolts removed by sinks, trades settled, the Citywide Charge, and (once the token layer opens) $AMP burned, the treasury balance, buybacks and the champions' purse.

Controls & commands

  • Click — walk, work a node, or talk to an NPC.
  • E — interact with whatever you're standing next to (trade, ride, spin).
  • 1–6 — tool belt / hotbar slots.
  • I pack · K skills · G goals · J Manifest · M minimap.
  • Enter — chat. Hold E for emotes.

Handy slash commands: /haul /park /near /crew /charge /help. The Manifest is your collection log — every first-find, craft and rare pays a one-time bonus and fills a page.

The rules we keep

These aren't marketing lines — they're built into the code and the ledger, and they don't bend:

✗never a wage ✗never pay-to-win ✗never printed ✗treasury never sold ✗no paid randomness

Free and premium Sparks share identical rates, stats, drop tables and stall slots. Progression comes from playing. $AMP buys breadth and identity — memberships gate content, never advantage. And no randomized outcome ever follows a payment, anywhere.

FAQ

How do I get in?

Connect an Ethereum wallet on Robinhood Chain and sign in. No email, no password — your wallet is your account. Or spectate the city read-only without a wallet, and connect when you want to play.

Do I need $AMP to play at launch?

No. At launch you just connect your wallet and play. Holding 1,000 $AMP becomes the key to the full city soon after — held in your wallet, never spent or taken. You keep all of it.

Can I turn Bolts into $AMP?

No. There is no path from Bolts, resources or items to the token. That wall is deliberate and permanent.

Does spending $AMP make me stronger?

No. $AMP buys cosmetics, memberships and stalls — identity and access. Power comes only from playing.

Is $AMP an investment?

No. $AMP is a utility token you use inside a game. This guide explains mechanism, not markets, and makes no promise about price.

How do I know the numbers are real?

The City Ledger publishes them monthly, in public, from the game's own records. Nothing is estimated.
